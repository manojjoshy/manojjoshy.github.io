Overview

This initiative aims to develop an innovative e-learning application specifically tailored for schools, providing students with engaging video-based content and comprehensive assessment tools. The platform is designed to enhance educational delivery in Afghanistan by offering a localized experience in Dari, Pashto, and English. My role in this project was to oversee the quality of the content, ensuring its relevance and accuracy, while also enhancing the visual quality of the product to ensure an engaging and aesthetically pleasing user experience.

Project Objectives

The objectives of the project focused on developing a localized and accessible e-learning application tailored for Afghan schools. The initiative aimed to enhance educational delivery by providing engaging video-based content, comprehensive assessment tools, and interactive features such as real-time chat, discussion boards, and collaborative tools. Designed to support multiple languages (Dari, Pashto, and English), the platform sought to address challenges like localization, role-based access management, and content control, while fostering interactivity and improving user experience to support better learning outcomes.

Localized Platform

The e-learning web app is designed to enhance educational delivery in Afghanistan by providing a localized platform in Dari, Pashto and English.

Centralized Content

Catering to schools, the app offers a centralized content system that aligns with curricula, role-based access and interactive features for assessments, chat and discussions.

Role-Based Access

The app offers role-based access for principals, teachers and students.

Interactive Features

The app includes interactive features for assessments, chat and discussions.

User Research

To ensure the e-learning application effectively met the needs of its users, we initiated comprehensive user research involving interviews and contextual inquiries. This research focused on understanding the specific challenges faced by principals, teachers, and students in Afghan schools. Key areas of investigation included usability, language preferences, role-based requirements, and the need for interactive features to enhance engagement. The insights gained from this process informed the design decisions, ensuring the platform was intuitive, culturally relevant, and aligned with the educational goals of its users.

Design Process

User Journeys

We created detailed user journeys for each user type, including principals, teachers, and students. These journeys mapped out the steps each user would take to interact with the application, such as accessing dashboards, managing content, completing assessments, and participating in discussions. By identifying user goals, challenges, and touchpoints, we ensured the design addressed their specific needs and provided a seamless experience. This process highlighted opportunities to streamline navigation, enhance role-based features, and integrate interactive tools, aligning the platform with the users' expectations and educational objectives.

Information Architecture

We designed the information architecture of the e-learning platform to ensure a clear and intuitive user experience tailored to each user type. Centralized dashboards were developed for principals, teachers, and students, providing quick access to essential functionalities such as content management, assessments, and communication tools. The structure prioritized easy navigation, with categorized menus and logical workflows to simplify tasks like uploading curriculum materials, managing assessments, and accessing learning resources. This well-organized architecture supported role-based access, multi-language usability, and interactive features, enabling users to efficiently achieve their objectives within the platform.
